## What ABQ Is

ABQ stands for **Always Be Quitting** (alwaysbequitting.com). It's Jon Fritsch's smoking and vaping cessation business. Jon is a certified tobacco treatment specialist who helps people quit smoking and vaping **permanently** through coaching, community support, and education.

The one-sentence version, in Jon's own framing: *"A lot of people want to quit smoking/vaping but they keep failing and going back to it. When they're tired of the cycle and want to quit for the last time, I'm the guy they call."* (voice-profile/storybrand_email_foundation…)

The core promise is not helping someone stop briefly. It's helping them **become someone who doesn't go back** — the difference between temporary abstinence and permanent quitting. (context.md; brand-voice.md)

## Who ABQ Serves

**Primary buyer hypothesis** (revised March 2026). Someone who:

- Has discretionary income (professional or financially stable)
- Is 30+ years old
- Any gender
- Has experienced real consequences from smoking/vaping — physical, financial, relational, or emotional
- Has tried to quit at least once and failed
- Has previously paid for professional help with a personal problem (coaching, therapy, personal training, weight-loss programs)

(context.md; brand-voice.md)

> **Note on a demographic conflict:** the copywriting voice guide names the primary audience as **women 40+** (secondary: men 40+), while the March 2026 context and brand-voice docs say **30+, any gender**. Both are in the vault. See `open-questions.md`.

**Discovery signal:** they publicly express discouragement about not being able to quit — in social media, forums, comments, and conversations. (context.md; brand-voice.md)

**The key psychological insight:** health risk alone does **not** drive someone to pay for help. The accumulated **discouragement from repeated failed attempts** is what opens the door. This audience already knows smoking/vaping is hurting them and has tried patches, gum, apps, cold turkey, and willpower. They don't need more information; they need support, accountability, and a better process. (context.md)

**Emotional state on arrival:** exhausted from repeated failures, ashamed but not wanting more shame, skeptical anything will work, hopeful but guarded. Many have started to believe something is wrong with *them* personally — not with their approach. (brand-voice.md; context.md)

### The four member stages (address all four)
1. **Thinking** — want to quit someday, keep putting it off
2. **Ready** — finally ready, want to set a quit date
3. **Already quit** — want to make sure it lasts
4. **Relapsed** — went back, want to get on track

(brand-voice.md; website/community-landing.md)

### Strategic secondary audiences (different validation stages)
Young adult vape / nicotine-pouch users (high need, strong proof/shareability, weaker direct payers); people recently quit and vulnerable to relapse; and B2B payers — employers, HR/wellness buyers, brokers, plus podcasts, speaking gigs, and doctor-referral partners. (ABQ Codex Context Pack/02-audience-and-customer.md; research/…/prism-committee-report.md; outreach/profiles/)

## The Problem ABQ Solves

The underlying problem isn't ignorance. It's repeated quit failure, addiction psychology, withdrawal avoidance, shame, low self-trust, and lack of structured support. ABQ reframes failed attempts as a **support/process problem, not a character flaw.** (context.md; brand-voice.md)

Jon's clinical reframes (from voice-profile/paradoxes-and-pain-points.md and the cold-turkey script):
- **Addiction, not habit.** Jon doesn't use "habit loop" language.
- **Transformation, not abstinence.** Real quitting = dopamine reset + mindset transformation over months.
- **97% of cold-turkey attempts fail.** If you've relapsed, you're the norm — "the 97% club" — not weak.
- **The fear of withdrawal is almost always worse than withdrawal itself.**

## Jon's Story (for About-page use)

Jon spent 25 years as a clinical tobacco treatment specialist and university educator, teaching future healthcare providers how to help people quit. He recently retired from teaching to build Always Be Quitting so he could help quitters directly — and, per the vault, partly to **support his family in retirement.** (context.md; chatterjee pitch; "I tell future doctors this…" email)

His origin insight, taught at the start of every lecture since 2011: everything about treating withdrawal, choosing methods, and success rates *"none of it matters if your client never does anything to change."* The first, biggest hurdle is simply starting. That belief — take action now, you don't have to feel ready — is the spine of ABQ's message. ("I tell future doctors this…" email)

His approach is evidence-based and warm, explicitly **not** willpower mythology or wellness "woo": *"No crystals, no mushrooms, no willpower mythology."* (paradoxes-and-pain-points.md)
