# ABQ — Voice & Writing Style

*How Jon writes and what the brand should sound like, with verbatim excerpts pulled from his own writing in the vault. Sources cited inline.*

---

## The Voice in One Line

**Empathetic friend + expert guide. Warm but professional, direct but kind.** It should sound like a 25-year expert who genuinely likes the people he helps — never generic wellness copy, never a shame-based lecture. (brand-voice.md; context.md)

The emotional order is always the same: **empathy first, hope second, solution third.** (context.md)

---

## Core Tone Rules

**Do:**
- Acknowledge the struggle without amplifying shame
- Reframe failure as "lack of the right support," not "lack of willpower"
- Pivot from problem to hope quickly (brief acknowledgment → reframe → solution)
- Be specific and tangible, not vague
- Sound human, not clinical; use plain language over medical jargon
- Position Jon as professional expert (empathy *then* credentials)

**Don't:**
- Fear-of-death or medical-scare messaging (they already know the risks)
- Long lists of consequences or shame spirals
- YouTuber hype ("this changes everything!", "X is dead")
- Attacking tobacco/vape companies by name
- Implying Jon was ever a smoker or vaper
- Over-medicalized language when plain words work

(brand-voice.md; ABQ_copywriting_voice_and_guide_and_rules.md)

**Personality seasoning:** Jon is sarcastic, witty, dry, occasionally cynical. That humor can *season* content but should not dominate conversion copy, which stays empathetic and transformation-focused. Example of the tone in the wild: "Accountability check-ins (voluntary and without shock collars 🙂)." (brand-voice.md; community-landing.md)

**Handle-with-care swaps** (use the right column):
- "slave to nicotine" → "feel controlled by smoking/vaping"
- "you failed" → "it didn't stick"
- "you feel ashamed" → "you're ready for something different"
- "wasting money" → "spending thousands yearly"
- "addiction running the show" → "ready to take back control"

(brand-voice.md; ABQ_copywriting_voice_and_guide_and_rules.md)

**A formatting note / internal contradiction:** the copywriting guide states **"NO EM DASHES. EVER."** — yet Jon's actual emails and scripts use dashes freely. Treat "no em dashes" as a stated *rule* but know his real writing doesn't follow it. Flagged in `open-questions.md`.

## Verbatim Excerpts (Jon's own writing)

These are lifted directly from the vault. Use them to calibrate tone, and as reusable copy blocks.

**1. The action-now philosophy** (email: "I tell future doctors this. Now I'm telling you.")
> "When it comes to quitting smoking or vaping, the most important question is: When? When are you going to quit? My answer is always the same. Now. Now is the time to start. You don't have to be ready. You don't have to have all the answers. You just have to take action."

**2. Small actions, frozen by fear** (email: "One small thing that moves quitting forward")
> "Here's what I see all the time: People want to quit smoking or vaping, but they don't do anything about it. Not because they don't care. But because fear and past failures keep them frozen. They think, 'I've tried before and it didn't work, so why bother?'… And they do nothing. For weeks. Months. Years. Decades."

**3. Momentum reframe** (same email)
> "Many small changes, which are easy to do, often add up to one big life change that once seemed impossible."

**4. Reframing withdrawal as strength** (Withdrawal production script)
> "EVERY SINGLE WITHDRAWAL SYMPTOM - from the earliest physical discomfort to those lingering challenges - is a sign that YOUR BRAIN IS GETTING STRONGER… Your brain isn't failing - it's beginning a remarkable REBUILDING PROCESS."

**5. The blunt-but-warm hook** (video script: "Why Cold Turkey Keeps Failing")
> "I know nothing about you. But if I had to make a bet, I'd bet you're going to fail the next time you try to quit smoking or vaping. Probably not what you expected to hear… It's not willpower. It's not weakness. Or the type of plan you choose."

**6. Temporary abstinence vs. transformation** (same script)
> "Because people achieve temporary abstinence, but they never transform. That's the difference between stopping for a few weeks and quitting for life… If you've tried multiple times and gone back, you're not weak. You're the norm. You're part of the 97 percent club."

**7. The Solo Paradox** (paradoxes-and-pain-points.md)
> "Your brain tells you needing help is weakness. But the same brain that got you addicted is the one you're asking to get you unaddicted. It's like asking a pickpocket to guard your wallet."

**8. The Minimization Paradox** (paradoxes-and-pain-points.md)
> "People convince themselves 'it's not that bad' while their brain actively hides the reality. One client thought he 'barely vaped' — he was hitting it 47 times a day."

**9. Making the reader feel seen** (email: "What's REALLY stopping you from quitting?")
> "Most people think they know. But when I ask them to write it down, they realize they've never actually named it. And you can't solve a problem you haven't clearly identified."

**10. Anti-credential-bragging honesty** (Welcome email)
> "I hate when newsletters spend five emails bragging about credentials. You should know about me and my qualifications, but I'm not going to bore you here."

**11. Reframing the whole relationship with smoking** (paradoxes-and-pain-points.md)
> From: "Smoking/vaping is something I love that I have to give up." To: "Smoking/vaping is something harmful I'm protecting myself from."

**12. The community value close** (community-landing.md)
> "You're already spending the money. You're just giving it to companies that make you sick. We don't compare ourselves to those companies; we are the antidote to those companies."

## The Transformation Promise (message architecture)

Every page should carry this distinction: **stopping is fast; staying quit is the real work.** Success is defined specifically and tangibly, never vaguely:
- Not going back (the primary success marker)
- Breathing freely without wheezing
- Thousands back in your pocket each year
- No more hiding or making excuses
- Proud instead of ashamed
- Free from cravings and control

## The StoryBrand Backbone

ABQ's messaging is built on Donald Miller's StoryBrand framework. Jon's completed seven "sound bites" (use these as canonical copy): (voice-profile/storybrand_email_foundation…)

1. **Character (what they want):** "Quit smoking and/or vaping for the last time"
2. **Problem:** "You keep trying to quit on your own but it's not working"
3. **Guide:** "I understand how discouraging this struggle is. I've helped all types of people quit successfully, and I can help you, too."
4. **Plan:** Join the community → start your first weekly challenge → build a toolbox of skills for during and after you quit
5. **Call to action:** "If you are struggling to quit smoking or vaping on your own, joining our flagship quitting community is the right decision"
6. **Success:** "You'll finally become the non-smoker and non-vaper you dreamed about"
7. **Failure:** "You can continue to spend $1k, $2k, $3k, $4k every year… and next year at this time, you'll wish you would have joined our community today."

**Rules that constrain the framework:** clear not clever; customer as hero, Jon as guide; specific not vague; ~75% problem / 25% product — but with *empathetic* problem acknowledgment, never traditional shame-based agitation. (storybrand foundation; ABQ_copywriting_voice_and_guide_and_rules.md)

## The One Publishing Test

Before anything ships, ask: *"Would a 45-year-old woman who's tried to quit 5 times and feels ashamed about still smoking feel understood — or worse — reading this?"* If worse, rewrite it. (brand-voice.md; ABQ_copywriting_voice_and_guide_and_rules.md)
